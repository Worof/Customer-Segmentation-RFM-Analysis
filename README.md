# 📊 Customer Segmentation using RFM Analysis

## 📌 Overview

This project applies **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Analysis** to segment customers based on their purchasing behavior. The objective is to transform transactional data into actionable customer insights that support personalized marketing, customer retention, and business growth.

Using Python, I cleaned the data, engineered RFM features, assigned scores, and mapped customers into meaningful marketing segments such as **Champions**, **Loyal Customers**, **Potential Loyalists**, **At Risk**, and **Can't Lose**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze customer purchasing behavior using RFM metrics.
- Segment customers into actionable groups.
- Identify high-value, loyal, and at-risk customers.
- Support data-driven marketing and retention strategies.

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains customer transaction records including:

- Customer ID
- Purchase Date
- Transaction Frequency
- Total Spending (Monetary Value)

---

## ⚙️ Project Workflow

1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
2. Feature Engineering
3. Calculate:
   - Recency
   - Frequency
   - Monetary Value
4. Assign RFM Scores (1–5)
5. Generate RFM Codes
6. Customer Segmentation
7. Data Visualization
8. Business Insights

# 💡 Key Insights

### 🏆 Champions
- Highest scores across Recency, Frequency, and Monetary.
- The most valuable and engaged customer segment.
- Ideal candidates for loyalty programs and exclusive offers.

---

### ❤️ Loyal Customers
- Purchase frequently and spend consistently.
- Strong retention potential.
- Suitable for upselling and cross-selling campaigns.

---

### 🌱 Potential Loyalists
- Recently active with promising purchasing behavior.
- Can be nurtured into loyal customers through personalized engagement.

---

### 🆕 New Customers
- Recently acquired but with limited purchase history.
- Onboarding campaigns can encourage repeat purchases.

---

### ⚠️ Need Attention
- Moderate purchase activity with declining engagement.
- Targeted promotions may help improve retention.

---

### 🚨 At Risk
- Largest customer segment in the dataset.
- Previously valuable customers whose activity has decreased.
- Requires proactive win-back campaigns before churn.

---

### ❌ Can't Lose
- High historical value but no recent purchases.
- High-priority reactivation segment.

---

### 😴 About to Sleep
- Low engagement and infrequent purchases.
- Gentle reminders and personalized offers may increase activity.

---

### 💤 Hibernating
- Long periods of inactivity with low purchase frequency.
- Lowest engagement among existing customers.

---

### 🌟 Promising
- Early-stage customers showing positive purchase behavior.
- Opportunity to increase engagement before they become inactive.

---
